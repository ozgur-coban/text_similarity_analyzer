Possible risks of orthognathic surgery
'As with other surgeries, jaw surgeries also carry certain risks. These can be simple risks or they can be deadly. It is very important that you understand well some of the risks that may develop after jaw tip surgery.

Surgery and other procedures to be performed: An incision will be made through the mouth or over the skin to reach the lower jaw bone. In addition, if it is necessary to take bone or tissue from another part of your body, it may be necessary to make an incision from another part of your body. The mandible will be corrected by cutting the bone at the appropriate place (osteotomy) and fixed using one or more of the following:

- Titanium plates and/or screws, fusible plate and/or screws, sutures, metal arches and wires applied to teeth, bone, cartilage and tissues taken from your body, bone, cartilage, bone paste, bone powder, silicone from another human or animal, alloplastic materials such as porous hydroxyapatite, methyl methacrylate, titanium mesh

Unless there is a problem, these materials can stay in place for a lifetime. If problems arise, a second surgery may be required to remove them.

At the end of the operation or later, the lower jaw and the upper jaw can be locked for 7-10 days (Intermaxillary fixation). During this period, only watery foods should be fed. If there is difficulty in breathing or nausea, the rubbers applied for the fixation may be cut.
  • 1.Side effects that may develop due to any surgical intervention:
    • a.Discomfort/post-operative pain from the surgery itself
    • b.Edema (Swelling)
    • c.Bleeding
    • d.Numbness
  • 2.Injury of branch of facial nerve (Facial nerve),
  • 3.Giving blood: Bleeding (in surgery or after surgery) that requires an autologous donor or blood transfusion may occur,
  • 4.Recurrence or need for additional procedures,
  • 5.Infection: The wound may recur, but it usually resolves with antibiotics and local treatment (with washing).,
  • 6.Ischemic necrosis (tissue death) may occur due to decreased blood flow.,
  • 7.Instability: Movement that should not be at the bone cut (osteotomy) line.,
  • 8.Titanium plates and screws used to fix the jaw after surgery can cause pain and/or infection, which in some stages requires surgical removal.,
  • 9.Decreased mouth opening,
  • 10. Very rarely, bleeding may occur at the base of the skull. This can lead to blindness. This situation is permanent.,
  • 11. Obese people have an increased risk of wound infection, chest infection, heart and lung complications (adverse condition), and thrombosis.,
  • 12. Wound healing is poor in diabetics, smokers, malnourished, obese patients and in some personal conditions. Significant scars may remain in external incisions.,
  • 13. Scar,
  • 14. Weight loss,
  • 15. If there is a jaw joint problem before the surgery, this can completely go away, decrease or sometimes increase.,
  • 16. Relapse,
  • 17. Additional surgical procedures may be required in case of any complications.
